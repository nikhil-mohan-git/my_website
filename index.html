About

With a rich background as a researcher in particle physics, I have devoted numerous years to the exploration of subatomic particles and the vast universe. During this journey, I had the privilege of contributing to two international collaborations - the T2K and JUNO experiments. My contributions were particularly notable in the development of the JUNO software, where I focused on creating a software framework for simulating realistic data sets, calibration data, and algorithms for generators and analysis studies.


Currently, I am working as a Research Data Analyst at Institute of Biological Information Processing (IBI) in Forschungszentrum Jülich, leveraging my analytical prowess to make significant strides in the captivating field of Neuroscience. I am at the forefront of data analysis and statistical inference initiatives, with a particular emphasis on signal transmission data used in Retinal Implants. This groundbreaking work is aimed at enhancing the vision of patients suffering from retinal degeneration. I am in charge of designing and developing a comprehensive software framework in Python, aimed at streamlining the processing, analysis, and modeling of large-scale and complex experimental data.


I am exploring new horizons in the world of data analytics and AI. I am in pursuit of a dynamic career that merges my programming skills with applied scientific research, and thus help drive organizations forward through data-driven insights. As a technology enthusiast, I am excited to contribute to this ongoing transformation and help shape the future of AI, which I believe holds incredible potential for good.


Aside from my professional interests, I am also passionate about football and movies. I strive to maintain diverse interests and activities outside of my professional pursuits. Whether in the office, on the football field, or in front of the TV screen, I am always eager to engage with the world around me, and continue learning and growing as a person.
